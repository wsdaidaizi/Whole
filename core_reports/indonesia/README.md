# Indonesia — IFLS

## Scope and design

The Indonesia component uses a temporally ordered attitude-only design: parental measures come from IFLS4 and adult-child outcomes from IFLS5. It covers religion, subjective welfare, risk/time preferences, and arisan participation. Political ideology and direct family/gender attitudes are not included in the locked package.

The pooled specification is:

\[
Y_{ir}=\alpha+\beta P_{ir}+\Gamma X_{ir}+\phi_r+\varepsilon_{ir}
\]

The locked controls are child age and sex, parent age and sex, and a mother indicator in pooled models. Parent-province fixed effects are included. Standard errors are clustered by parent-origin kabupaten/kota; survey weights are not used.

## Core results

| Domain/outcome | Standardized parent coefficient | SE | Approx. N |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Self-rated religiosity | 0.049 | 0.007 | 15,970 |
| Economic-ladder assessment | 0.145 | 0.011 | 15,949 |
| Family-life assessment | 0.070 | 0.007 | 16,026 |
| Living-standard assessment | 0.077 | 0.005 | 16,033 |
| Overall-life assessment | 0.028 | 0.004 | 16,047 |
| Arisan participation | 0.083 | 0.012 | 16,653 |
| Risk-preference money choice | 0.001 | 0.003 | 15,925 |
| One-year time preference | 0.001 | 0.002 | 16,001 |
| Five-year time preference | -0.001 | 0.001 | 16,001 |

Positive resemblance is clearest for economic position, social participation, family/living-standard assessments, and religiosity. Risk and time preferences show little detectable parent–child association under the locked specification.

## Education sensitivity

Adding child and parent grade-completion controls changes the focal coefficients only modestly in the main religion and subjective-welfare domains. These columns are sensitivity analyses, not causal mediation tests.

## Interpretation

Indonesia demonstrates strong domain specificity: family resemblance is visible in religion, subjective welfare, and social participation, but not across every preference measure. The evidence should not be described as a universal transmission coefficient.
